# Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL)

Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL) states that the total current entering a junction is equal to the total current leaving the junction.

<img src="assets/animations/kcl.svg" alt="Kirchhoff's Current Law Animation">

---

## Formula

`∑I(in) = ∑I(out)`

or

`∑I = 0`

---

## Key Points

- Based on the conservation of electric charge
- Applied at electrical junctions (nodes)
- Used for nodal analysis
- Valid for both DC and AC circuits

---

## Simple Understanding

Current cannot accumulate at a junction. Whatever current enters a node must leave it.
