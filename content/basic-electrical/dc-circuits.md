# DC Circuits

A DC (Direct Current) circuit is an electrical circuit in which current flows in only one direction. DC circuits are commonly powered by batteries, solar cells, and DC power supplies.

<img src="assets/animations/dc-circuits.svg" alt="DC Circuits Animation">

---

## Characteristics

- Current flows in one direction only.
- Voltage remains constant over time.
- Commonly used in electronic devices and battery-operated systems.

---

## Basic Formulas

### Ohm's Law

`V = I × R`

### Electrical Power

`P = V × I`

### Electrical Energy

`E = P × t`

## Key Points

- Uses constant voltage and current.
- Easier to analyze than AC circuits.
- Widely used in portable electronic devices.
- Forms the basis for learning electrical circuit analysis.

---

## Simple Understanding

A DC circuit is like water flowing continuously in one direction through a pipe without changing its direction.
