# Fundamentals of Electricity

Electricity is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. It is the foundation of all electrical and electronic systems.

---

## Basic Electrical Quantities

### Electric Charge (Q)

Electric charge is the basic property of matter responsible for electrical effects.

**Unit:** Coulomb (C)

---

### Electric Current (I)

Current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor.

**Unit:** Ampere (A)

---

### Voltage (V)

Voltage is the electrical potential difference between two points that causes current to flow.

**Unit:** Volt (V)

---

### Resistance (R)

Resistance is the opposition offered by a material to the flow of electric current.

**Unit:** Ohm (Ω)

---

### Power (P)

Electrical power is the rate at which electrical energy is converted into other forms of energy.

**Unit:** Watt (W)

Formula:

`P = V × I`

---

### Energy (E)

Electrical energy is the total work done by an electrical system over time.

**Unit:** Joule (J) or Kilowatt-hour (kWh)

Formula:

`E = P × t`

---

## Key Points

- Electricity is caused by the movement of electrons.
- Voltage pushes current through a circuit.
- Resistance limits current flow.
- Power represents the rate of energy consumption.
- Energy is power used over time.

---

## Simple Understanding

Think of electricity like water flowing in a pipe:

- Voltage → Water pressure
- Current → Water flow
- Resistance → Narrowness of the pipe
- Power → Speed of doing work
