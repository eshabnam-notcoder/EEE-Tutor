# AC Circuit Analysis

AC circuit analysis is the study of electrical circuits supplied with alternating current. In AC circuits, resistance, inductance, and capacitance affect the flow of current.

<img src="assets/animations/ac-circuit-analysis.svg" alt="AC Circuit Analysis Animation">

---

## Circuit Elements

### Resistance (R)

Opposes the flow of current.

**Unit:** Ohm (Ω)

---

### Inductive Reactance (XL)

Opposition offered by an inductor to AC.

Formula:

`XL = 2πfL`

---

### Capacitive Reactance (XC)

Opposition offered by a capacitor to AC.

Formula:

`XC = 1 / (2πfC)`

---

### Impedance (Z)

The total opposition to AC current.

Formula:

`Z = √(R² + (XL − XC)²)`

**Unit:** Ohm (Ω)

---

## Power in AC Circuits

### Active Power

`P = VI cosθ`

**Unit:** Watt (W)

---

### Reactive Power

`Q = VI sinθ`

**Unit:** VAR

---

### Apparent Power

`S = VI`

**Unit:** VA

---

### Power Factor

`Power Factor = cosθ`

---

## Key Points

- Impedance replaces resistance in AC analysis.
- Inductors and capacitors affect current differently.
- Power factor indicates how efficiently electrical power is used.
- AC circuit analysis is essential for power systems and electrical machines.

---

## Simple Understanding

In AC circuits, electricity not only faces resistance but also additional opposition from inductors and capacitors. Together, this total opposition is called **impedance**.
