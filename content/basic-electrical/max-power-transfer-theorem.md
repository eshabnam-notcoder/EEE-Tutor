# Maximum Power Transfer Theorem

The Maximum Power Transfer Theorem states that maximum power is delivered to the load when the load resistance equals the source (Thevenin) resistance.

<img src="assets/animations/maximum-power.svg" alt="Maximum Power Transfer Animation">

---

## Formula

`RL = Rth`

Maximum Power:

`Pmax = (Vth²) / (4Rth)`

---

## Key Points

- Used in communication and electronic circuits
- Provides maximum power to the load
- Efficiency at maximum power is 50%
- Based on Thevenin equivalent circuits

---

## Simple Understanding

A load receives the highest possible power when its resistance exactly matches the source resistance.
