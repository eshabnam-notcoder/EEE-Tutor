# Power Generation, Transmission, and Distribution

Electrical power systems generate electricity, transmit it over long distances, and distribute it safely to homes, industries, and businesses.

<img src="assets/animations/power-system.svg" alt="Power System Animation">

---

## Power Generation

Electricity is generated using various energy sources.

Examples:

- Thermal Power Plant
- Hydroelectric Power Plant
- Nuclear Power Plant
- Solar Power Plant
- Wind Power Plant

---

## Power Transmission

Generated electricity is transmitted at very high voltage to reduce power losses over long distances.

---

## Power Distribution

Electricity is delivered from substations to consumers at safe voltage levels.

---

## Main Components

- Power Plant
- Transformer
- Transmission Lines
- Substation
- Distribution Lines

---

## Key Points

- High-voltage transmission reduces losses.
- Transformers change voltage levels.
- Distribution delivers usable electricity to consumers.
- Forms the backbone of modern electrical infrastructure.

---

## Simple Understanding

Electricity is generated at power plants, carried across long distances using transmission lines, and finally delivered safely to homes and industries through distribution networks.
