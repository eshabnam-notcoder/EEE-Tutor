# Magnetic Circuits

A magnetic circuit is a closed path through which magnetic flux flows. It is similar to an electrical circuit, but magnetic flux replaces electric current.

<img src="assets/animations/magnetic-circuit.svg" alt="Magnetic Circuit Animation">

---

## Important Terms

### Magnetic Flux (Φ)

The total magnetic field passing through a surface.

**Unit:** Weber (Wb)

---

### Magnetomotive Force (MMF)

The force that drives magnetic flux through a magnetic circuit.

Formula:

`MMF = N × I`

---

### Reluctance (ℜ)

Opposition offered by a magnetic path to magnetic flux.

Formula:

`ℜ = l / μA`

---

## Key Points

- Similar to electrical circuits.
- Flux flows through magnetic materials.
- Used in transformers and electrical machines.
- Iron provides a low-reluctance path.

---

## Simple Understanding

A magnetic circuit is like an electrical circuit where magnetic flux flows instead of electric current.
