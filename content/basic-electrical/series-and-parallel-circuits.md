# Series and Parallel Circuits

Electrical components can be connected in two basic ways: series connection and parallel connection.

<img src="assets/animations/series-parallel-circuits.svg" alt="Series and Parallel Circuits Animation">

---

## Series Circuit

In a series circuit, components are connected one after another, providing only one path for current.

### Formula

`Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...`

---

## Characteristics

- Same current flows through every component.
- Voltage is divided among components.
- If one component fails, the entire circuit stops working.

---

## Parallel Circuit

In a parallel circuit, all components are connected across the same two terminals, providing multiple paths for current.

### Formula

`1/Rtotal = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...`

---

## Characteristics

- Same voltage appears across every branch.
- Current divides among branches.
- If one branch fails, the remaining branches continue to operate.

---

## Key Differences

| Series Circuit | Parallel Circuit |
|----------------|------------------|
| One current path | Multiple current paths |
| Same current | Same voltage |
| Voltage divides | Current divides |
| Total resistance increases | Total resistance decreases |

---

## Key Points

- Series circuits are simple but less reliable.
- Parallel circuits are commonly used in homes and industries.
- Equivalent resistance depends on the type of connection.
- Understanding series and parallel circuits is essential for circuit analysis.

---

## Simple Understanding

In a series circuit, electricity has only one road to travel. In a parallel circuit, electricity has multiple roads to reach its destination.
