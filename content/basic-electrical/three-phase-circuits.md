# Three-Phase Circuits

A three-phase circuit consists of three alternating voltages of equal magnitude and frequency that are 120° apart. It is the most common system used for power generation, transmission, and industrial applications.

<img src="assets/animations/three-phase-circuits.svg" alt="Three Phase Circuits Animation">

---

## Types of Connections

### Star (Y) Connection

- Three phase windings are connected to a common neutral point.
- Can provide both phase and line voltages.

---

### Delta (Δ) Connection

- Windings are connected end-to-end forming a closed loop.
- Does not require a neutral wire.

---

## Basic Formulas

### Star Connection

`VL = √3 × Vph`

`IL = Iph`

---

### Delta Connection

`VL = Vph`

`IL = √3 × Iph`

---

## Key Points

- Provides constant power.
- Requires less conductor material.
- Efficient for large electrical loads.
- Widely used in industries and power systems.

---

## Simple Understanding

Instead of one AC supply, three AC supplies work together with a 120° phase difference, providing smoother and more efficient power.
