# AC Fundamentals

Alternating Current (AC) is an electric current that periodically changes both its magnitude and direction. It is the standard form of electricity supplied to homes and industries.

---

## Important Terms

### Alternating Current (AC)

Current that reverses its direction continuously.

---

### Frequency (f)

The number of complete cycles produced in one second.

**Unit:** Hertz (Hz)

---

### Time Period (T)

The time required to complete one cycle.

Formula:

`T = 1 / f`

---

### Peak Value

The maximum value of voltage or current.

---

### RMS Value

The effective value of AC voltage or current.

Formula:

`Vrms = Vmax / √2`

`Irms = Imax / √2`

---

### Phase

The angular position of one waveform with respect to another.

---

## Waveform

The most common AC waveform is a sine wave.

---

## Key Points

- AC continuously changes direction.
- Standard frequency is 50 Hz in many countries.
- RMS values are used for practical calculations.
- AC can be easily transmitted over long distances.

---

## Simple Understanding

Unlike DC, AC keeps changing its direction many times every second, making it ideal for power transmission.
