# Biomass Power Plant

A biomass power plant generates electricity by burning organic materials such as plant waste, wood, and agricultural residues.

<img src="assets/animations/biomass-power-plant.svg" alt="Biomass Power Plant Animation">

---

## Fuel Used

- Wood chips
- Agricultural waste
- Animal waste
- Biogas

---

## Main Components

- Boiler
- Combustion Chamber
- Steam Turbine
- Generator
- Condenser
- Fuel Handling System

---

## Working Principle

1. Biomass fuel is burned in a boiler.
2. Heat produces steam.
3. Steam rotates a turbine.
4. Turbine drives the generator.
5. Electricity is produced.

---

## Advantages

- Renewable energy source
- Reduces waste
- Carbon neutral (approx.)
- Locally available fuel

---

## Disadvantages

- Lower efficiency than fossil fuels
- Fuel collection and storage issues
- High maintenance
- Requires large fuel supply

---

## Applications

- Rural power generation
- Small industries
- Waste-to-energy plants
- Agricultural regions

---

## Key Points

- Uses organic waste as fuel
- Converts chemical energy to electrical energy
- Helps in waste management
- Renewable but less efficient

---

## Simple Understanding

A biomass power plant burns natural waste materials to produce heat and generate electricity.
