# 1. Introduction

The increasing demand for electricity, depletion of fossil fuels, and growing environmental concerns have accelerated the adoption of renewable energy sources worldwide.

Renewable energy is energy obtained from naturally replenishing resources such as sunlight, wind, flowing water, biomass, and geothermal heat. Unlike fossil fuels, these resources are continuously available and produce significantly lower greenhouse gas emissions.

Modern power systems are gradually transitioning from centralized fossil-fuel-based generation to cleaner and more sustainable energy systems. However, renewable energy sources are variable and intermittent, making their integration into the electrical grid a challenging task.

Renewable Energy Integration refers to the process of connecting renewable energy sources with the existing power system while maintaining reliable, stable, secure, and economical operation.

---

# Definition

**Renewable Energy Integration** is the process of incorporating electricity generated from renewable energy sources into the electrical power system through appropriate generation, power electronic interfaces, communication, control, and protection systems.

# 3. Wind Energy

Wind energy is the kinetic energy possessed by moving air. A wind turbine converts this kinetic energy into mechanical energy, which is then converted into electrical energy using a generator.

Wind energy is one of the fastest-growing renewable energy sources because it is clean, abundant, and suitable for large-scale electricity generation.

---

# Principle of Wind Energy Conversion

The rotating blades drive a shaft connected to an electrical generator, producing electricity.

- High-speed shaft
- Generator

- Nacelle

---

# Working of a Wind Turbine

1. Wind strikes the rotor blades.
2. The blades rotate due to aerodynamic forces.
3. The rotating shaft transfers mechanical energy to the gearbox.
4. The gearbox increases the rotational speed.
5. The generator converts mechanical energy into electrical energy.
6. The generated electricity is stepped up by a transformer and supplied to the grid.

---

# Power Available in Wind

The theoretical power available in wind is given by

\[
P=\frac{1}{2}\rho A V^3
\]

where,

- \(P\) = Wind power (W)
- \(\rho\) = Air density (kg/m³)
- \(A\) = Swept area of turbine blades (m²)
- \(V\) = Wind velocity (m/s)

### Observations

- Wind power is proportional to the cube of wind speed.
- Even a small increase in wind speed produces a significant increase in power.
- Larger blade diameter increases the swept area and hence power generation.

---

# Betz Limit

A wind turbine cannot extract all the kinetic energy from the wind.

According to **Betz's Law**, the maximum theoretical efficiency of a wind turbine is

\[
\eta_{\text{max}} = 59.3\%
\]

Practical wind turbines generally achieve efficiencies between **35% and 45%**.

---

- Most commonly used

## 2. Vertical Axis Wind Turbine (VAWT)

The rotor axis is perpendicular to the ground.

Features:

- Can accept wind from any direction
- Simpler construction
- Lower efficiency
- Suitable for small applications

# 6. Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy is the heat energy stored beneath the Earth's surface. This heat originates from the Earth's core and the radioactive decay of naturally occurring elements.

The heat is used to produce steam, which drives a turbine connected to an electrical generator.

Geothermal power plants provide continuous (base-load) electricity generation because the Earth's internal heat is available throughout the year.

---

# Working Principle of a Geothermal Power Plant

Hot water or steam from underground reservoirs is brought to the surface through production wells.

The steam rotates a turbine, which drives a generator to produce electricity.

After use, the cooled water is reinjected into the reservoir to maintain sustainability.

# Synchronization Conditions

Before connecting an AC generator to the grid, the following conditions must be satisfied:

- Equal voltage magnitude
- Equal frequency
- Same phase sequence
- Zero phase angle difference

Failure to synchronize properly may damage equipment and disturb the power system.

# 9. Energy Storage Systems (ESS)

Renewable energy sources such as solar and wind are intermittent because their output depends on weather conditions.

Energy Storage Systems (ESS) store excess electrical energy during periods of high generation and supply it back to the grid during periods of high demand or low renewable generation.

Thus, ESS improves the reliability, stability, and flexibility of renewable energy-based power systems.

---

# Need for Energy Storage

Energy storage is required because:

- Solar energy is unavailable at night.
- Wind speed varies continuously.
- Electricity demand changes throughout the day.
- Renewable generation may exceed demand during certain periods.
- Grid stability requires continuous balance between generation and load.

---

# Functions of Energy Storage

- Peak load support
- Load leveling
- Frequency regulation
- Voltage support
- Renewable energy balancing
- Backup power supply
- Black start capability
- Improved power quality

Pumped Hydro Energy Storage is the most widely used large-scale energy storage technology.

# 9.3 Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES)

In CAES systems, excess electricity compresses air and stores it in underground caverns or storage tanks.

When electricity is required, the compressed air expands through a turbine to generate power.

# 9.4 Flywheel Energy Storage

A flywheel stores energy in the form of rotational kinetic energy.

An electric motor accelerates a heavy rotating wheel during charging.

During discharge, the rotating flywheel drives a generator to produce electricity.

# 13. Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT)

Renewable energy sources such as solar PV operate at different power outputs depending on environmental conditions.

Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) is a control technique used to extract maximum available power from renewable energy systems.

---

# MPPT in Solar PV

The output of a solar PV panel depends on:

- Solar irradiance
- Temperature
- Load conditions

The MPPT controller continuously adjusts the operating point to obtain maximum power.

# 15. Grid Codes for Renewable Energy Integration

Grid codes define technical requirements that renewable energy plants must satisfy before connecting to the electrical grid.

They ensure safe and reliable operation.

---

# Important Grid Code Requirements

## 1. Voltage Regulation

Renewable plants must maintain acceptable voltage levels at the connection point.

---

## 2. Frequency Response

Generators must support system frequency during disturbances.

---

## 3. Fault Ride Through (FRT)

Renewable generators should remain connected during short-duration faults instead of disconnecting immediately.

---

## 4. Reactive Power Control

Renewable plants should provide reactive power support when required.

---

## 5. Power Quality Requirements

The injected power must satisfy limits on:

- Harmonics
- Flicker
- Voltage variations
