# Wind Power Plant

A wind power plant generates electricity by converting the kinetic energy of wind into mechanical energy using wind turbines.

<img src="assets/animations/wind-power-plant.svg" alt="Wind Power Plant Animation">

---

## Main Components

- Wind Turbine Blades
- Rotor
- Gear Box
- Generator
- Tower
- Anemometer
- Yaw System

---

## Working Principle

1. Wind blows over turbine blades.
2. Blades rotate due to wind energy.
3. Rotor turns the shaft.
4. Gearbox increases rotational speed.
5. Generator produces electricity.
6. Power is transmitted to the grid.

---

## Types

- Onshore wind farms
- Offshore wind farms

---

## Advantages

- Renewable energy source
- No pollution
- Low operating cost
- Sustainable energy production

---

## Disadvantages

- Depends on wind speed
- High installation cost
- Noise pollution
- Requires large land/space

---

## Applications

- Wind farms
- Rural electrification
- Power grids
- Offshore energy projects

---

## Key Points

- Converts wind energy into electricity
- Works best in high wind regions
- Intermittent energy source
- Needs backup systems for stability

---

## Simple Understanding

Wind turbines work like large fans that rotate due to wind and generate electricity.
