# Solar Power Plant

A solar power plant generates electricity by converting sunlight directly into electrical energy using photovoltaic (PV) cells.

<img src="assets/animations/solar-power-plant.svg" alt="Solar Power Plant Animation">

---

## Main Components

- Solar Panels (PV Cells)
- Inverter
- Battery (optional)
- Charge Controller
- Mounting Structure
- Transformer

---

## Working Principle

1. Sunlight falls on solar panels.
2. PV cells convert sunlight into DC electricity.
3. Inverter converts DC into AC.
4. Power is supplied to the grid or stored in batteries.

---

## Types

- Grid-connected solar plant
- Off-grid solar plant
- Hybrid solar plant

---

## Advantages

- Renewable energy source
- No fuel cost
- Low maintenance
- Environment friendly

---

## Disadvantages

- Depends on sunlight availability
- High initial cost
- Requires large area
- Energy storage needed for night use

---

## Applications

- Residential power supply
- Street lighting
- Remote areas
- Solar farms

---

## Key Points

- Uses photovoltaic effect
- Clean and sustainable energy
- Efficiency depends on sunlight intensity
- Inverter is essential for AC supply

---

## Simple Understanding

A solar power plant works like a panel that captures sunlight and converts it into usable electricity.
