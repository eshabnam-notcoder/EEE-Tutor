# Gas Turbine Power Plant

A gas turbine power plant generates electricity by using high-pressure hot gases to rotate a turbine connected to a generator.

<img src="assets/animations/gas-turbine-power-plant.svg" alt="Gas Turbine Power Plant Animation">

---

## Fuel Used

- Natural Gas
- Diesel
- Kerosene

---

## Main Components

- Compressor
- Combustion Chamber
- Gas Turbine
- Generator
- Air Intake
- Exhaust System

---

## Working Principle

1. Air is compressed in the compressor.
2. Fuel is injected and burned in the combustion chamber.
3. High-temperature gases are produced.
4. Hot gases expand and rotate the turbine.
5. Turbine drives the generator.
6. Exhaust gases are released.

---

## Advantages

- Quick start-up.
- Compact design.
- Lower installation cost than thermal plants.
- Suitable for peak load demand.

---

## Disadvantages

- Low efficiency compared to other plants.
- High fuel cost.
- High exhaust temperature losses.
- Not suitable for continuous base-load operation.

---

## Applications

- Peak load power plants
- Aircraft engines (jet turbines)
- Emergency power supply
- Remote power generation

---

## Key Points

- Works on Brayton cycle.
- Uses compressed air and fuel combustion.
- High-speed rotating turbine.
- Best for short-duration power demand.

---

## Simple Understanding

A gas turbine plant works like a jet engine where burning fuel produces hot gases that spin a turbine to generate electricity.
