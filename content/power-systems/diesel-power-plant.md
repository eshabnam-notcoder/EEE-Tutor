# Diesel Power Plant

A diesel power plant generates electricity using a diesel engine that drives an alternator.

<img src="assets/animations/diesel-power-plant.svg" alt="Diesel Power Plant Animation">

---

## Fuel Used

- Diesel (High-speed diesel oil)

---

## Main Components

- Diesel Engine
- Fuel System
- Air Intake System
- Exhaust System
- Cooling System
- Lubrication System
- Alternator (Generator)

---

## Working Principle

1. Diesel fuel is injected into the engine cylinder.
2. Air-fuel mixture is compressed and ignited.
3. Combustion produces high-pressure gases.
4. Piston moves and rotates the crankshaft.
5. Crankshaft drives the alternator.
6. Alternator generates electricity.

---

## Advantages

- Simple design.
- Quick start and shutdown.
- Low initial cost.
- Suitable for small power demands.

---

## Disadvantages

- High running cost due to fuel.
- Noise and vibration.
- Limited power output.
- Not suitable for large-scale generation.

---

## Applications

- Emergency backup power
- Hospitals
- Small industries
- Remote locations
- Construction sites

---

## Key Points

- Uses internal combustion engine.
- Converts chemical energy into mechanical and then electrical energy.
- Highly portable compared to other plants.
- Mostly used for standby power.

---

## Simple Understanding

A diesel power plant works like a car engine connected to a generator that produces electricity when fuel is burned.
