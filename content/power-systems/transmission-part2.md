# Transmission Systems - Part 2

Transmission line performance depends on electrical parameters such as resistance, inductance, and capacitance.

<img src="assets/animations/transmission-part2.svg" alt="Transmission Parameters Animation">

---

## Line Parameters

### Resistance (R)

Opposes flow of current and causes power loss.

---

### Inductance (L)

Opposes change in current and affects voltage drop.

---

### Capacitance (C)

Causes charging current in transmission lines.

---

### Conductance (G)

Represents leakage current.

---

## Effects on Transmission Lines

- Voltage drop
- Power loss
- Efficiency reduction

---

## Key Points

- Parameters depend on conductor material
- Length and spacing affect performance
- Important for line design

---

## Simple Understanding

Transmission lines behave like a combination of resistance, inductance, and capacitance that affects how electricity flows.
