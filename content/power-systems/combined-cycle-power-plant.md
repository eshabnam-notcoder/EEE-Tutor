# Combined Cycle Power Plant

A combined cycle power plant generates electricity using both gas and steam turbines to improve overall efficiency.

<img src="assets/animations/combined-cycle-power-plant.svg" alt="Combined Cycle Power Plant Animation">

---

## Main Components

- Gas Turbine
- Heat Recovery Steam Generator (HRSG)
- Steam Turbine
- Generator
- Condenser
- Fuel System

---

## Working Principle

1. Fuel is burned in the gas turbine.
2. Gas turbine generates electricity.
3. Hot exhaust gases are sent to HRSG.
4. HRSG produces steam using waste heat.
5. Steam drives a steam turbine.
6. Additional electricity is generated.

---

## Advantages

- Very high efficiency (up to ~60%)
- Better fuel utilization
- Lower emissions per unit power
- Cost-effective for large plants

---

## Disadvantages

- High initial cost
- Complex system design
- Requires skilled operation
- Longer setup time

---

## Applications

- Large power plants
- Industrial power generation
- Utility-scale electricity production

---

## Key Points

- Combines gas and steam cycles
- Uses waste heat recovery
- Higher efficiency than simple gas plants
- Common in modern power stations

---

## Simple Understanding

A combined cycle plant produces electricity twice—first using a gas turbine, then using leftover heat to produce more power with a steam turbine.
