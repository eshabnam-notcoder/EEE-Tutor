# Nuclear Power Plant

A nuclear power plant generates electricity by using heat released from nuclear fission reactions.

<img src="assets/animations/nuclear-power-plant.svg" alt="Nuclear Power Plant Animation">

---

## Fuel Used

- Uranium (U-235)
- Plutonium (Pu-239)

---

## Main Components

- Nuclear Reactor
- Fuel Rods
- Control Rods
- Moderator
- Coolant
- Steam Generator
- Turbine
- Generator
- Containment Structure

---

## Working Principle

1. Nuclear fission occurs in the reactor core.
2. Heat is produced during fission.
3. Coolant carries heat to the steam generator.
4. Water converts into steam.
5. Steam rotates the turbine.
6. Turbine drives the generator.
7. Electricity is produced.

---

## Advantages

- Very high energy output.
- Low fuel requirement.
- No greenhouse gas emissions during operation.
- Suitable for large-scale power generation.

---

## Disadvantages

- Radioactive waste disposal problem.
- High construction cost.
- Risk of radiation hazards.
- Complex safety requirements.

---

## Applications

- Base-load power plants
- Large-scale electricity production
- Space and research applications

---

## Key Points

- Energy is released from atomic nuclei.
- Controlled chain reaction is essential.
- Extremely high energy density.
- Requires strict safety measures.

---

## Simple Understanding

A nuclear power plant produces electricity by releasing energy from atoms, similar to controlled mini explosions that generate heat to produce steam.
