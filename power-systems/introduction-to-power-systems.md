# Introduction to Power Systems

A power system is a network of electrical components that generates, transmits, distributes, and utilizes electrical energy. It ensures a continuous and reliable supply of electricity to consumers.

<img src="assets/animations/power-system-introduction.svg" alt="Power System Introduction Animation">

---

## Main Components

### Power Generation

Electricity is produced in power plants using various energy sources such as coal, water, nuclear fuel, solar energy, and wind energy.

---

### Power Transmission

Electricity is transmitted over long distances using high-voltage transmission lines to reduce power losses.

---

### Power Distribution

Electricity is delivered from substations to homes, industries, and commercial consumers through distribution networks.

---

### Electrical Loads

Loads are devices or equipment that consume electrical energy, such as lights, motors, heaters, and electronic appliances.

---

## Basic Structure

Power Plant → Step-up Transformer → Transmission Line → Substation → Distribution Line → Consumer

---

## Objectives of a Power System

- Generate electrical energy efficiently.
- Transmit power with minimum losses.
- Distribute electricity safely.
- Maintain reliability and stability.
- Provide uninterrupted power supply.

---

## Advantages

- Supplies electricity over large areas.
- Supports industrial and economic growth.
- Enables efficient long-distance power transmission.
- Provides reliable electrical service.

---

## Key Points

- A power system consists of generation, transmission, distribution, and utilization.
- High-voltage transmission reduces transmission losses.
- Transformers are used to change voltage levels.
- Protection systems ensure safe operation.

---

## Simple Understanding

A power system is like a transportation network for electricity. Power plants produce electricity, transmission lines carry it over long distances, and distribution lines deliver it safely to consumers.
