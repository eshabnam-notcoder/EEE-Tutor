# Thermal Power Plant

A thermal power plant generates electricity by converting the heat energy produced from burning fuel into electrical energy.

<img src="assets/animations/thermal-power-plant.svg" alt="Thermal Power Plant Animation">

---

## Fuels Used

- Coal
- Natural Gas
- Oil
- Lignite

---

## Main Components

- Boiler
- Steam Turbine
- Generator
- Condenser
- Cooling Tower
- Feed Water Pump
- Chimney

---

## Working Principle

1. Fuel is burned in the boiler.
2. Heat converts water into high-pressure steam.
3. Steam rotates the turbine.
4. The turbine drives the generator.
5. The generator produces electricity.
6. Steam is condensed back into water and reused.

---

## Advantages

- Large-scale power generation.
- Reliable continuous operation.
- Mature and well-established technology.
- Suitable for base-load power plants.

---

## Disadvantages

- Causes air pollution.
- High fuel consumption.
- Requires large amounts of cooling water.
- Produces greenhouse gases.

---

## Applications

- National power grids
- Industrial power generation
- Large cities

---

## Key Points

- Converts heat energy into electrical energy.
- Steam turbine is the prime mover.
- Coal is the most commonly used fuel.
- Requires efficient pollution control systems.

---

## Simple Understanding

A thermal power plant works like a giant steam engine. Fuel heats water to produce steam, which spins a turbine connected to a generator that produces electricity.
