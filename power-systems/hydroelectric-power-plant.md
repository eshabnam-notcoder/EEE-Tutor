# Hydroelectric Power Plant

A hydroelectric power plant generates electricity by converting the energy of flowing or falling water into electrical energy.

<img src="assets/animations/hydroelectric-power-plant.svg" alt="Hydroelectric Power Plant Animation">

---

## Main Components

- Dam
- Reservoir
- Penstock
- Water Turbine
- Generator
- Transformer
- Spillway

---

## Working Principle

1. Water is stored in a reservoir behind a dam.
2. Water flows through the penstock.
3. Flowing water rotates the turbine.
4. The turbine drives the generator.
5. The generator produces electricity.
6. Electricity is stepped up by a transformer for transmission.

---

## Advantages

- Renewable source of energy.
- No fuel cost.
- Low operating and maintenance costs.
- Produces very little air pollution.
- High efficiency.

---

## Disadvantages

- High initial construction cost.
- Depends on water availability.
- Large land requirement.
- Can affect aquatic ecosystems and nearby communities.

---

## Applications

- National power generation
- Peak load power supply
- Irrigation projects
- Flood control

---

## Key Points

- Uses the potential energy of stored water.
- Water turbines convert hydraulic energy into mechanical energy.
- Clean and renewable source of electricity.
- One of the most efficient methods of power generation.

---

## Simple Understanding

A hydroelectric power plant uses the force of falling water to spin a turbine, which turns a generator and produces electricity.
